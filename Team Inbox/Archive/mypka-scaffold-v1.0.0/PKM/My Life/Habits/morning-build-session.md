# Morning Build Session

**Why I do it:** the first focused block of the day is the most honest one. No meetings have eroded it, no inbox has hijacked it. If I spend it building, the rest of the day stays pulled forward instead of pushed around.

**Cadence:** weekdays, 90 minutes, starts within 30 minutes of sitting down at the desk.

**What counts as done:**
- one concrete artifact moved forward (a commit, a doc, a decision written down)
- no email, no Slack, no chat tools open during the block
- a one-line note at the end of the block answering "what got moved"

**Yardstick:** at week's end, I should be able to name five artifacts the morning sessions produced. Three is acceptable. Two means the habit slipped and needs a reset.

**Linked work:**
- supports [[ship-mvp-by-q3]] directly - this is where the MVP gets built
- feeds [[side-project-mvp]] when the day's session is pointed at it
- ties into [[ai-tooling]] when I use the block to test or refine a tooling workflow

**Notes I want to remember:** if I skip three days in a row, the recovery is harder than the original habit. Better to do a short, low-quality session than skip and reset.

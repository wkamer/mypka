*The critical initial stage to identify and confirm a product's market viability and profit potential.*

- **Phase 1: Discovery (Idea Generation & Trend Spotting)**

   - **Objective:** To cast a wide net and identify potential product ideas and emerging market trends.

   - **Key Actions:**

      - Niche selection (based on interest, passion, or identified problems).

      - Broad market scanning (Google Trends, Exploding Topics, social media trends).

      - Brainstorming common pain points and desires.

      - Initial Browse of e-commerce best-seller lists (Amazon, AliExpress, Etsy).

- **Phase 2: Validation (Demand & Profitability Proof)**

   - **Objective:** To rigorously test if a discovered product has sufficient demand, a clear problem-solution fit, and strong profit margins.

   - **Key Actions:**

      - Deep dive into keyword research (specific product search volumes).

      - Analyze specific product sales data on marketplaces (AliExpress orders, competitor sales estimates).

      - Conduct a thorough cost analysis (product + shipping + payment fees + ad cost).

      - Determine realistic selling price and target profit margin.

      - Assess Average Order Value (AOV) potential through upsells/cross-sells.

      - **Order a sample product from the supplier** (crucial for quality, functionality, and actual shipping times).

- **Phase 3: Competitor Analysis (Understanding the Landscape & Finding Your Edge)**

   - **Objective:** To learn from existing players, identify market gaps, and define your unique selling proposition (USP).

   - **Key Actions:**

      - Identify direct and indirect competitors.

      - Analyze competitor product pages, pricing, and bundle offers.

      - Deconstruct competitor advertising strategies (Facebook Ad Library, TikTok, Google Ads).

      - **Deep dive into customer reviews (especially 3-star reviews)** for competitor products to find pain points, unmet needs, and opportunities for differentiation.

      - Define your unique selling proposition (USP) – what makes your product or offer better/different.

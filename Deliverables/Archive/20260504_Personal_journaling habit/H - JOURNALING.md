# H - JOURNALING

## What this habit is

Daily journaling — writing down what's on my mind. Not for productivity. For clarity, pattern recognition, and self-knowledge. Penn processes and stores every entry.

---

## Why it matters

The owner's core pattern (KE - SELF) is waiting until things feel right before moving. Journaling is the tool that makes patterns visible. You can't fix what you can't see. This habit feeds directly into self-awareness and the shift from stalling to moving.

---

## The trigger

**When:** Morning — before opening the phone or starting work. Or evening — right after dinner, before anything else.
**Where:** At the desk. Pen optional, phone to Claude Code accepted.
**Duration:** 5 minutes minimum. No maximum. 5 is enough.

---

## The minimum viable entry

Three questions. Answer at least one. That is a complete entry.

1. What's on my mind right now?
2. What did I do yesterday that I'm proud of?
3. What is one thing I want to move on today?

One honest sentence per question is enough. Progress over perfection.

---

## How it connects to the system

Every entry goes to **Penn** (Journal Agent). Say "journal this" or paste your text — Penn handles storage, tagging, and linking to relevant life buckets. You don't manage the files. You just write.

Penn path: `Personal/PKM/My Life/Journal/YYYY/MM/`

---

## The rule

**Write something. Anything. Every day.**
A single sentence counts. Skipping because it's not "good enough" is exactly the pattern we're breaking.

---

## Streak tracking

Not tracked obsessively. If you miss a day: write the next day. No recovery entry needed. Just continue.

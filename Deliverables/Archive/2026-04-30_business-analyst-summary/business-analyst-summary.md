# Business Analyst Summary

A Business Analyst (BA) in a software company acts as the bridge between business stakeholders and the technical team. Their primary job is to understand what the business needs, translate those needs into clear requirements, and ensure the development team builds the right thing. This involves running discovery sessions with stakeholders, documenting functional and non-functional requirements, mapping existing processes, and identifying gaps or inefficiencies that software can solve. The BA owns the requirements throughout the project lifecycle — clarifying ambiguities, managing scope, and making sure nothing falls through the cracks between what was asked for and what gets built.

On the delivery side, a BA in a software company works closely with product managers, developers, QA engineers, and sometimes clients to validate that what is being built matches what was agreed upon. They write user stories, acceptance criteria, and process flows that give the development team unambiguous targets to build against. In agile environments, the BA often sits inside the sprint cycle, answering questions in real time and refining the backlog. The measure of a good BA is whether the team ships something the business actually wanted — with minimal rework and no surprises at handover.

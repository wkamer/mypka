# Top 5 Must-See in Brussels

1. **Grand Place** — The central square, ringed by ornate guild houses and the Town Hall. UNESCO World Heritage Site. Go at night for the lighting.

2. **Atomium** — The giant steel atom structure built for the 1958 World's Fair. Iconic, quirky, and uniquely Brussels. Views over the city from the top.

3. **Manneken Pis** — The tiny, famous statue of a urinating boy. Small but worth seeing — he's often dressed in costume. Symbol of Brussels' self-deprecating humor.

4. **St. Michael & St. Gudula Cathedral** — The main cathedral of Belgium. Gothic, grand, and where royal ceremonies are held. Worth stepping inside.

5. **Musée Magritte / Royal Museums of Fine Arts** — Home to the world's largest collection of René Magritte's surrealist work. Part of a larger museum complex.

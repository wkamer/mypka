# Bryan Johnson Blueprint — Exercise Protocol Reference

> Clean reference material. All content sourced from Bryan Johnson's own publications.
> Last updated: 2026-05-29

---

## Overview

Bryan Johnson exercises 6 hours per week, 6 days per week, typically in a single morning session of 60 to 90 minutes beginning at 6:00 AM. Each session combines elements from all 5 pillars. Weekly heart rate distribution targets:

| Zone | BPM range | Weekly target |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Zone 1 (light) | ~88 to 105 | 1 hour |
| Zone 2 to 4 (moderate) | ~106 to 159 | 4.5 hours |
| Zone 5 (vigorous) | 159+ | 1.5 hours |

Guiding principle: "Do not give yourself the option to decide." Exercise is a non-negotiable daily input.

---

## Pillar 1 — Strength Training

**Frequency:** 3 dedicated sessions per week (strength also embedded in daily sessions)
**Session duration:** 30 to 45 minutes of the daily 60 to 90 minute block

### Lower Body

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Poliquin Step Ups | 3 x 10 per leg | Performed in circuit with slant board squats and ATG split squats |
| Slant Board Squats | 3 x 10 to 15 | Slant board required; knees-over-toes emphasis |
| ATG Split Squats | 3 x 10 per leg | Full range; part of the same lower body circuit |
| Nordic Curls | 1 x 10 | Hamstring eccentric emphasis; GHD or Nordic bench |
| Reverse Nordics | 1 x 10 | Quad eccentric; kneel on pad, lean back controlled |
| Seated Calf Raises | 1 to 3 x 25 | Machine or with plate on knees |
| Leg Raises | 1 x 50 | Core/hip flexor; hanging or lying |
| Back Extensions | 1 x 25 | Hyperextension bench; weighted (25 to 45 lb plate) |
| Oblique Extensions | 1 x 25 per side | Side-lying on hyperextension bench |

### Upper Body

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Pull Ups | 1 x 15 | Overhand grip |
| Chin Ups | 1 x 15 | Underhand grip |
| Inverted Rows / Reverse Push Ups | 1 x 25 | Rings or bar; body at angle |
| Face Pulls | 1 x 15 | Cable or band; external rotation emphasis |
| Chest Fly / Butterfly | 1 x 15 | Cable machine or resistance bands |
| Band Pull Aparts | 1 x 15 | Behind and in front of body |
| Tricep Extensions | 1 x 25 | Cable pushdown or kickback; 1-sec hold at top |
| Bicep Curls | 1 x 15 | Dumbbells |
| External Shoulder Rotation | 1 x 15 per arm | Dumbbell side-lying or cable low |

### Posture / Supplemental (2x per week only)

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Levator Scapula Shrug | 1 x 30 | 20 lb dumbbells; 2x per week maximum |
| Suboccipital Stretch | 1 x 10 | 30 degree angle; avoid overextension |

### Circuit Structure Note

The lower body strength block (Poliquin Step Ups, Slant Board Squats, ATG Split Squats) is performed as a continuous circuit: complete one set of each, then repeat. No rest between exercises within a round; 30 seconds between rounds.

### Equipment Required

- Slant board (for squats)
- Nordic/GHD bench (for Nordics and Reverse Nordics)
- Hyperextension bench (for back extensions and obliques)
- Cable machine or resistance bands (face pulls, flys, tricep, external rotation)
- Pull up bar
- Dumbbells (various)
- Sled (for warm up)

### Progression

Progressive overload is a core principle. Aim to gradually increase difficulty over time. Form takes priority over load. Johnson does not publicly publish a specific periodization scheme; the consistent application of progressive overload on every movement is the stated method.

## Pillar 2 — Zone 2 Cardio (Low Intensity)

**Target:** 150 minutes per week of moderate activity at Zone 2
**Heart rate target:** 60 to 70% of maximum heart rate (approximately 106 to 140 BPM for most adults)
**Conversation test:** You can hold a conversation, but breathing is elevated
**Frequency:** 4 days per week, 30 to 60 minutes per session

### Modalities (choose one per session)

- Stationary bike / cycling
- Elliptical trainer
- Rowing machine
- Brisk walking
- Jogging at easy pace
- Swimming
- Hiking (weekends: Johnson does 5.5 km with 365m elevation gain)

### Protocol Structure

1. Warm up: 5 to 10 minutes at low effort (Zone 1, below 105 BPM)
2. Steady state: 30 to 50 minutes maintaining 60 to 70% max HR
3. Cool down: 5 minutes easy

### Notes

Zone 2 specifically trains mitochondrial efficiency. Johnson targets 4.5 hours total per week in Zones 2 to 4 combined. Weekend hiking counts toward this total. Pickleball, basketball, and tennis also contribute to Zone 2 to 4 volume.

## Pillar 3 — Zone 4 to 5 Cardio / High Intensity (Norwegian 4x4)

**Target:** 75 minutes per week of vigorous exercise (Zone 5, 159+ BPM)
**Frequency:** 3 sessions per week (Monday, Wednesday, Friday in Johnson's schedule)
**Session duration:** 25 to 40 minutes total including warm up

### The 4x4 Protocol (Johnson's primary HIIT method)

Bryan Johnson described this himself on X (December 2024):

> "For my 4x4 high intensity training (HIIT), I did 4 minutes of all out exertion on an elliptical followed by 4 minutes of rest. Do that four times. You can do this on a bike, running, swimming, etc."

Source: [Bryan Johnson on X, December 2024](https://x.com/bryan_johnson/status/1871278967984185599)

| Phase | Duration | Intensity |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Warm up | 5 to 10 min | 130 to 140 BPM (Zone 2) |
| Work interval | 4 minutes | All out, 85 to 95% max HR (Zone 4 to 5) |
| Rest interval | 4 minutes | 60 to 70% max HR (active recovery) |
| Rounds | 4 total | Total work time: 16 minutes |
| Cool down | 5 min | Easy pace |

Total session: approximately 30 to 40 minutes.

### Modalities

- Elliptical (Johnson's primary choice)
- Indoor bike / cycling ergometer
- Rowing machine
- Treadmill running
- Outdoor running
- Swimming

### Alternative HIIT Structure (shorter version used at times)

Some sources document a compressed variant used in daily sessions:

- 5 to 10 min warm up at 130 to 140 BPM
- 5 min HIIT interval on bike or elliptical
- Repeat structure depending on session goal

### Norwegian 4x4 Research Basis

The 4x4 structure derives from research by Prof. Jan Hoff at NTNU Norway. The standard protocol: 4 x 4 minutes at 85 to 95% of max HR with 3-minute active recovery between intervals. Johnson uses a 4-minute rest variant which is more conservative than the original 3-minute research protocol.

### Progression

- Start with 4 to 6 rounds at a manageable intensity
- Build to consistent Zone 4 to 5 effort during all work intervals
- Do not increase rounds before you can sustain target HR throughout all intervals

## Pillar 4 — Mobility and Flexibility

**When performed:** Integrated into every session (warm up and cool down); dedicated block on flexibility-focus days
**Duration:** 10 to 30 minutes depending on session type

### Daily Mobility Routine (performed every session)

| Exercise | Duration / Reps | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Kneeling Shin Stretch | 30 to 60 sec per leg | Knee on pad; press shin toward floor |
| Hip Flexor Stretch | 30 to 60 sec per leg | Low lunge position; keep pelvis level |
| Couch Stretch | 30 to 60 sec per leg | Back foot on bench/wall; quad and hip flexor |
| Hamstring Stretch | 30 to 60 sec | Use platform or weight for depth if needed |

### Dynamic Warm Up Mobility (before strength or HIIT)

| Exercise | Reps / Duration | Notes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| Hip CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations) | 5 per side | Full-range hip circles, slow and controlled |
| Wall Slides | 10 reps | Back flat against wall; arms slide up/down |
| Thread the Needle | 5 per side | Thoracic rotation |
| Arm Circles | 10 each direction | Shoulder warm up |
| Lunge with Torso Rotation | 5 per side | Dynamic hip and thoracic mobility |

### Flexibility / Yoga Block (Day 3 and Day 7)

| Exercise | Duration | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Cat-Cow | 10 reps | Spinal flexion and extension |
| Cobbler's Pose | 30 to 60 sec | Inner groin and hip opener |
| Pigeon Pose | 30 to 60 sec per side | Deep hip external rotation |
| Cobra Pose | 30 sec | Lumbar extension; gentle |
| Downward Dog | 30 to 60 sec | Hamstrings, calves, shoulders |
| Tree Pose | 30 sec per side | Balance and hip opening |
| Standing Figure-Four Stretch | 30 sec per side | Glute and hip external rotator |
| Jefferson Curl | 5 x 30 sec | Loaded spinal flexion; light weight only |
| Pancake Stretch | 30 to 60 sec | Wide-stance seated forward fold |

### Notes

Johnson includes light yoga or full-body stretching on recovery days (Day 7) for 20 to 30 minutes. The couch stretch and kneeling shin stretch are core daily fixtures; he does not skip them even on short sessions.

## Pillar 5 — Balance

**When performed:** Warm up segment of every session; also embedded in posture/stability work
**Duration:** 5 to 10 minutes per session

### Daily Balance and Ankle Stability

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Tibialis Raises | 1 to 3 x 20 to 25 | Back against wall, toes lift up; strengthen tibialis anterior |
| IsoTib Ankle Rotations | 1 to 3 x 10 to 15 per side | Isolated ankle rotation under load; IsoTib device or improvised |
| Single-Leg Stance | 30 sec per side | Eyes open; progress to eyes closed |
| Bird Dog | 1 x 10 per side | Core and balance; slow and controlled |

### Intermediate Balance Progression

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Single-Leg RDL | 1 to 3 x 10 per side | Light dumbbell; hip hinge on one leg |
| Single-Leg Toe Touch | 1 x 10 per side | Balance and posterior chain |
| Heel-to-Toe Walking | 20 steps | Straight line; arms extended |
| Tree Pose (yoga) | 30 sec per side | Single-leg yoga balance |

### Advanced Balance

| Exercise | Sets x Reps | Notes |
|----------|-------------|-------|
| Pistol Squat | 3 x 5 per side | Single-leg squat to full depth |
| Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift | 3 x 10 per side | Weighted; maintain flat back |
| Stability Ball Plank | 1 x 30 to 60 sec | Forearms on ball; anti-extension balance |
| Overhead Squat | 3 x 5 | Mobility and balance combined |

### Equipment

- IsoTib device (for ankle rotations; can substitute with bodyweight)
- Stability ball
- Flat open floor for single-leg work

### Notes

Tibialis raises and IsoTib ankle rotations appear in every session. Johnson emphasizes ankle and lower-leg health as foundational for all other movement. These exercises address a commonly neglected segment of longevity training.

## Weekly Schedule Summary

| Day | Primary Focus | Key Content |
|-----|--------------|-------------|
| Monday | HIIT + Strength | 4x4 HIIT on elliptical/bike; lower body strength circuit |
| Tuesday | Zone 2 + Strength | 30 to 45 min Zone 2 cardio; upper body strength |
| Wednesday | HIIT + Strength | 4x4 HIIT; lower body circuit |
| Thursday | Zone 2 + Flexibility | 30 to 45 min Zone 2 cardio; mobility and yoga block |
| Friday | HIIT + Strength | 4x4 HIIT; full body strength |
| Saturday | Active Recovery / Zone 2 | Hiking, pickleball, basketball, or easy cycling |
| Sunday | Active Recovery | Gentle yoga, stretching, mindfulness |

Every session begins with: backwards sled (2 min), mobility warm up, then main work.

## Key Principles from Johnson

1. "Exercise everyday, no matter what (unless injured or under doctor's orders)."
2. "Progressive overload is key. Aim to gradually increase difficulty over time."
3. "Form before resistance." Never sacrifice range of motion for load.
4. "Do not give yourself the option to decide." Remove the daily decision entirely.
5. Injury avoidance is the top priority: "Injuries can create serious limitations."
6. After every meal: 5 to 10 minutes of light activity.
7. Every 30 minutes at desk: get up and move.

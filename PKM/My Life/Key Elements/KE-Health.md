# HEALTH

## What this means for me

Health is a conscious life driver — not a goal to reach but a dimension to maintain and build over time. The body is the foundation everything else is carried by.

## Bryan Johnson's vision — the overarching philosophy

Source: "Don't Die" — systems-level personal alignment, not motivation.

The core shift: from **YOLO** (live now, ignore consequences) to **Don't Die** (build a body and life that carries you forward for decades). Not about aesthetics. About capability, independence, and being fully engaged with your life at 60, 80, and 100.

Key insight: most people aren't stuck because they don't know what to do. They're stuck because they're comfortable. Motivation gives you the feeling of progress without the cost of change. What actually creates change is self-trust — keeping small promises to yourself, day by day.

"Pick one small thing. Do it today. Do it again tomorrow. Do it when it's boring. Do it when you don't feel like it."

## The 8-step framework (systems-level alignment)

**Step 1 — Do hard things**
Nothing changes until you're willing to be uncomfortable. Most of what holds you back isn't reality — it's the stories you repeat. Take ownership of everything: the good and the bad.

**Step 2 — The end is the beginning (sleep)**
Your morning starts the night before. Sleep is the #1 priority above everything else. Wind-down ritual without exceptions:
- Finish last meal at least 4 hours before bed
- Lower lights, eliminate blue light, shut off screens
- Read, walk, breathe, stretch, meditate
- Go to bed at the same time every night

**Step 3 — Start your day with purpose (morning routine)**
- Wake up at the same time every day
- Get bright light in your eyes (sunlight or full spectrum)
- Move your body: walk, stretch, breathe — just enough to shift from passive to present
- Consistency creates stability; stability gives you leverage over your day

**Step 4 — Future proof your body (exercise)**
Movement is non-negotiable daily. Not for aesthetics — for capability. See the five pillars below.

**Step 5 — Treat food as medicine**
Food becomes your health or your illness over time. The food system is designed to keep you hungry and sick.
- Prioritize: whole foods, clean protein, unprocessed carbs, fruits, vegetables, healthy fats
- Reduce aggressively: ultra-processed foods, added sugar, artificial additives, alcohol, smoking
- Rule of thumb: "All food is guilty until proven innocent"
- Tired, unmotivated, moody? Poor food choices are almost always the culprit

**Step 6 — Kill the distractions**
Smartphones = a slot machine in your pocket. Ultra-processed content is junk food for the mind: stimulating but leaves you scattered, anxious, reactive.
- Set limits: time caps, app blockers, fewer notifications
- Decide what earns your attention and what doesn't
- Goal: protect your clarity

**Step 7 — Remove isolation**
Chronic loneliness is as harmful as smoking a pack a day. Humans thrive on connection — on being seen, heard, understood.
- Prioritize relationships: friends, family, love
- Real conversations, shared meals, walks together
- "We weren't meant to do life alone"

**Step 8 — Stop watching motivational garbage**
Motivation gives you the feeling of progress without the cost of change. Real change comes from self-trust: keeping small promises to yourself, little by little, day by day.
- No more big declarations, no more identity overhauls that last a week
- Pick one small thing. Do it. Repeat.

## Step 4 in detail — the five exercise pillars

Source: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JNuORofHhrk

**1. Strength training**
- Min. 3x per week, compound movements: push, pull, squat
- Progressive overload: add weight or reps week over week
- Goal: functional strength — "strength today is independence tomorrow"
- Muscle = metabolic armor: stabilizes joints, improves insulin sensitivity, supports bone density

**2. Zone 2 cardio (low intensity)**
- 150 min/week — walking, jogging, cycling, swimming
- Pace: can still hold a full conversation
- Improves mitochondrial function, burns fat efficiently
- Quick win: 10-min walk after meals drastically reduces glucose response

**3. Zone 4-5 cardio (high intensity)**
- 75 min/week, heart rate 80-90% of max
- VO2 Max = strongest single predictor of longevity; each unit improvement = +45 days life expectancy
- Lowest 25th percentile VO2 Max: 5x higher mortality risk vs top 2%
- Gold standard: Norwegian 4x4 — 4 min zone 5, 3 min zone 2, repeat 4 sets

**4. Mobility and flexibility**
- 5-10 min/day minimum
- Focus areas: hips, spine, ankles, hamstrings, shoulders
- Falls = leading cause of injury and death in older adults; mobility prevents this
- Pilates recommended

**5. Balance**
- Daily practice: stability drills, slow controlled movements, single-leg stance (eyes closed)
- Research: unable to balance on one leg for 10 sec = 84% higher all-cause mortality risk
- Reflects neurological health, coordination, brain health

Bryan's personal protocol: 6-8 hours/week — 30 min strength + 30 min cardio daily, mobility/balance layered throughout the day.

## Core principles

- Consistency over perfection
- Movement is not about looking good — it is about building a body to carry forward
- Small steps compound over decades
- Self-trust is built by keeping small promises, not through motivation
- Data-driven: measure, adjust, improve

## What I want more of

- Functional mobility and flexibility
- Cardiovascular endurance built sustainably
- Strength that supports everyday life
- A daily movement habit that does not feel like effort
- Sleep as a non-negotiable foundation

## What I want less of

- Periods of inactivity that are hard to restart from
- Waiting until motivation arrives before moving
- All-or-nothing thinking about exercise
- Ultra-processed food and content

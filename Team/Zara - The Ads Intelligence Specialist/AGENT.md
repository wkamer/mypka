## Domain Knowledge

### Eugene Schwartz — The Foundation

Zara operates from Schwartz's core principle: **you do not create desire, you channel what already exists.** The market determines the angle, not the product. Every creative decision starts here.

**5 Awareness Levels — determines every word of copy:**
1. **Unaware** — does not know they have a problem. Hook: reveal the hidden pain.
2. **Problem Aware** — knows the problem, not the solution. Hook: consequences of ignoring it.
3. **Solution Aware** — knows solutions exist, not yours. Hook: why your solution type wins.
4. **Product Aware** — knows your product, not convinced. Hook: proof, objection handling.
5. **Most Aware** — ready to buy, needs a push. Hook: scarcity, urgency, final offer.

Wrong awareness level = irrelevant ad, regardless of copy quality.

**Market Sophistication Stages 1–5:**
- Stage 1–2: simple promise works. "Lose weight fast."
- Stage 3: differentiation and proof required. "Lose weight without cardio."
- Stage 4–5: unique mechanism + identity. "The cortisol-reset protocol women over 40 use."

The sophistication stage determines the claim strength. Overclaiming in a Stage 2 market wastes budget on skepticism. Underclaiming in a Stage 5 market blends into noise.

### Awareness × Funnel Matrix

25 intersections that define which angle works at which moment:

| Awareness | Awareness (Funnel) | Interest | Consideration | Intent | Conversion |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaware** | Problem creation | – | – | – | – |
| **Problem Aware** | Problem urgency | Introduce solutions | – | – | – |
| **Solution Aware** | – | Solution comparison | Position your solution | – | – |
| **Product Aware** | – | – | Product explanation | Objection handling | – |
| **Most Aware** | – | – | – | Final push | Close the deal |

### Creative Intelligence

**Hook categories (5 groups, 21 types):**
- Problem-Focused: Problem, Agitate, Question, Myth, Fear
- Desire-Focused: Benefit, Outcome, Vision, Identity, Lifestyle
- Credibility-Focused: Statistic, Contradiction, Authority, Proof, Guarantee
- Curiosity-Focused: Secret, Open Loop, Shock, What-if, Challenge
- Social-Focused: Relatable, Tribal, Trend, Comparison, Social Proof

**Hook is 80% of the result.** If they do not stop scrolling, the ad does not exist.

**Eén-regel testformule** — situational fit test before production:
> "In [tijd], in een [context], gebruikt iemand dit [gebruik] omdat het [frictie] of [emotie] oplevert."
If the sentence does not feel right, the hook does not fit.

**Creative fatigue signals — recognize before ROAS drops:**
- Frequency >2.5 per week for the same audience
- CTR declining >20% week-over-week
- Hook rate (3-second video views / impressions) declining >30%
- When two of three signals appear: rotate creative immediately

### Campaign Structure

**CBO vs. ABO:**
- CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization): for scaling proven winners and broad prospecting. Meta distributes budget to best-performing ad sets automatically.
- ABO (Ad Set Budget Optimization): for controlled creative testing. Fixed budget per ad set isolates variables.

**Funnel architecture:**
- Prospecting (70%): broad targeting, exclude buyers + warm audiences 180d
- Retargeting (25%): WV/ATC/IC/VC 180d — warm audiences who did not convert
- Retention (5%): purchasers 180d — repeat purchase, upsell, bundle

**Graduation System:**
1. Test: ABO, small budget, 6–8 creatives, 3–5 days minimum
2. Prove: creative holds ROAS >breakeven for 5+ days, consistent CPA
3. Scale: duplicate to CBO, increase budget 20–30% per step, not more

Never scale what is not proven. Never force what is not working.

**Kill/keep rules:**
- Kill: CTR <1% AND no ATC after ~1,500 impressions
- Kill: hook rate <15% after 1,000+ impressions
- Keep: ROAS >breakeven for 3+ consecutive days
- Duplicate winner: exact copy, new angle variant or thumbnail

### Market Intelligence

**Market Sophistication Mapper** — use before angle strategy:
- Analyze Ad Library: how many competitors, how long creatives run, which claims dominate
- Stage 1–2: fresh market, basic promise works
- Stage 3: differentiation required, proof matters
- Stage 4–5: unique mechanism, identity, transformation — generic claims invisible

**Competitor Ad Library analysis:**
- Creative running >3 weeks = likely winner, study the angle
- Many similar claims = market is saturated at that angle
- New angle appearing from multiple competitors = market is shifting

**Transformation framework:**
- Current State: what the customer's frustrating present situation is (in their words)
- Future State: what they aspire to
- Transformation Gap: the specific shift the product enables — this is the core of the ad

### Feedback Loop System

After every test cycle:
1. **Winner analysis**: why did this work? Which hook type, which awareness level, which angle?
2. **Loser analysis**: why did this fail? Wrong awareness level? Saturated angle? Creative fatigue?
3. **Hook database update**: categorize winning hooks by type + niche + awareness level
4. **Market learning**: which angles are saturating? What has not been tested yet?

The feedback loop compounds. A specialist who does not build this system starts every product from scratch.

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| Hook Rate (3s views/impressions) | >25–30% | <15% = kill |
| CTR | >1–3% | <1% after 1,500 impr. = kill |
| CPC | €0,30–€1,00 | Context-dependent |
| ROAS | >2–3x (breakeven) | Below breakeven 5+ days = pause |
| Frequency | <2,5/week | >2,5 = creative fatigue risk |
| CPA | Know breakeven CPA per product | Above breakeven = not scaling |
